# City Select

A city recommendation tool that scores and ranks cities based on your personal priorities — whether you're looking for a place to live or somewhere to visit.

## How it works

1. **Choose a mode** — Living or Visiting, or jump straight to a side-by-side city comparison
2. **Rank your priorities** — drag dimensions into the order that matters to you
3. **Take a short quiz** — answers tune the scoring (climate preferences, career focus, budget, language, etc.)
4. **See your results** — top 5 ranked cities with scores, strengths, and weaknesses, plus a full ranked table of all cities

Scoring is a weighted average across 27 dimensions (safety, cost of living, air quality, transit, food scene, etc.). Your priority ranking and quiz answers adjust the weights.

---

## Running locally

The app is plain HTML/CSS/JS with no build step. It uses ES modules, so it must be served over HTTP (not opened as a `file://` URL).

```bash
# Python 3 (simplest)
python serve.py
# then open http://localhost:8000

# Or any static server:
npx serve .
```

## Updating the data

City scores are compiled from a mix of authoritative datasets and hand-curated values. The pipeline lives in `data/`.

### Step 1 — Download sources

```bash
cd data
python sources/download.py
```

This fetches what can be pulled automatically:

| File | Source | Dimensions |
|------|--------|------------|
| `ti_cpi.csv` | World Bank WGI (Control of Corruption) | `corruption` |
| `sipri.csv` | World Bank (Military Expenditure % GDP) | `militarySpending` |
| `henley.csv` | Passport Index open dataset (GitHub) | `visaEase` |
| `climate_data.csv` | NASA POWER climate normals API | `sunshine`, `humidity`, `rainfall`, `winterWarmth` |

The script also prints instructions for the 7 dimensions that require manual downloads (Numbeo, IQAir, RSF, etc.) — see `data/sources/README.md` for details on each.

### Step 2 — Compile cities.csv

```bash
python data/build.py
```

This merges all source files into `data/cities.csv`. Missing source files fall back to the values in `manual_scores.csv` with a warning — so the pipeline is always runnable even with partial data.

### Editing manual scores

`data/sources/manual_scores.csv` is the baseline for all 27 dimensions. Dimensions that have no single authoritative downloadable source (transit quality, food scene, nightlife, cultural offerings, healthcare, etc.) live here permanently. Edit this file to correct or update any score, then rerun `build.py`.

## Adding a new city

1. Add a row to `data/sources/city_meta.csv` — include `id`, `name`, `country`, `region`, `population`, `climate`, `industries`, `primaryLanguage`, `gdpPerCapita`, `density`, `nickname`, `lat`, `lon`
2. Add a row to `data/sources/manual_scores.csv` — include scores for all 27 dimensions
3. Add city name variants to `data/sources/city_mappings.csv` (for Numbeo and IQAir matching)
4. Run `python data/build.py`

City IDs must be camelCase with no spaces (e.g. `newYork`, `kualaLumpur`).

## Dimensions

27 dimensions, all scored 0–100 (higher = better). Country-level dimensions apply the same score to every city in that country. City-level dimensions are unique per city.

Weights and rankability are defined in `data/dimensions.csv`. Scores are compiled by `data/build.py` — automated sources are fetched by `download.py`, everything else lives in `data/sources/manual_scores.csv`.

### Automated — fetched by `download.py`

| Dimension | Level | Source | How it's scored |
|-----------|-------|--------|-----------------|
| **corruption** | country | [World Bank WGI](https://info.worldbank.org/governance/wgi/) — Control of Corruption estimate | WGI value (−2.5 to +2.5) normalized: `(val + 2.5) / 5 × 100` |
| **militarySpending** | country | [World Bank](https://data.worldbank.org/indicator/MS.MIL.XPND.GD.ZS) — military expenditure % of GDP | Inverted: `100 − clamp(pct / 5 × 100)`. 0% GDP → 100, 5%+ → 0 |
| **visaEase** | country | [Passport Index](https://github.com/ilyankou/passport-index-dataset) open dataset | Count of visa-free + visa-on-arrival destinations, normalized to 193 max |
| **sunshine** | city | [NASA POWER](https://power.larc.nasa.gov/) — multi-year solar irradiance climatology | Annual mean solar irradiance (kWh/m²/day) converted to ~sunshine hours, then `clamp((hours − 1000) / 2500 × 100)` |
| **humidity** | city | NASA POWER — relative humidity climatology | Annual mean RH %: `clamp((rh − 30) / 55 × 100)`. 30% → 0, 85% → 100 |
| **rainfall** | city | NASA POWER — precipitation climatology | Annual total mm: `clamp(mm / 2500 × 100)`. 0mm → 0, 2500mm → 100 |
| **winterWarmth** | city | NASA POWER — temperature climatology | January mean °C: `clamp((jan + 20) / 45 × 100)`. −20°C → 0, +25°C → 100 |

### Manual download required — see `data/sources/README.md`

These sources require a manual export from their website. Once placed in `data/sources/`, `build.py` picks them up automatically.

| Dimension | Level | Source | How it's scored |
|-----------|-------|--------|-----------------|
| **pressFreedom** | country | [RSF World Press Freedom Index](https://rsf.org/en/index) | Score 0–100 (higher = more free). Used directly. |
| **costOfLiving** | city | [Numbeo Cost of Living Index](https://www.numbeo.com/cost-of-living/rankings.jsp) | NYC baseline ≈ 100. Inverted and normalized to observed range — lower cost → higher score |
| **affordability** | city | [Numbeo Price-to-Income Ratio](https://www.numbeo.com/property-investment/rankings.jsp) | Higher ratio = less affordable. Inverted and normalized |
| **safety** | city | [Numbeo Crime Index](https://www.numbeo.com/crime/rankings.jsp) | `100 − crime_index`. Lower crime → higher score |
| **airQuality** | city | [IQAir World Air Quality Report](https://www.iqair.com/world-air-quality-report) — city PM2.5 | `clamp((50 − pm25) / 50 × 100)`. WHO guideline is 5 µg/m³; at 50 µg/m³ score hits 0 |
| **internetQuality** | country | [Ookla Speedtest Global Index](https://www.speedtest.net/global-index) — median fixed broadband Mbps | `clamp(mbps / 200 × 100)`. 200+ Mbps → 100 |
| **languageAccess** | city | [EF English Proficiency Index](https://www.ef.com/wwen/epi/) | English-primary cities → 100. Others use country EPI score directly (0–100) |

### Hand-curated — lives in `manual_scores.csv`

These dimensions have no single authoritative downloadable source, or are inherently editorial. Scores are researched manually and stored in `data/sources/manual_scores.csv`. The reference materials used are listed below — edit the CSV directly to update any score.

| Dimension | Level | Reference material |
|-----------|-------|--------------------|
| **transitQuality** | city | [UITP Mobility in Cities Database](https://www.uitp.org/); TomTom Traffic Index; metro/rail network coverage |
| **climateAction** | country | [Climate Action Tracker](https://climateactiontracker.org) country ratings; national emissions targets |
| **govProgressiveness** | country | [Freedom House Freedom in the World](https://freedomhouse.org/report/freedom-world); [V-Dem Liberal Democracy Index](https://v-dem.net/) |
| **economicOutlook** | country | [IMF World Economic Outlook](https://www.imf.org/en/Publications/WEO); World Bank GDP growth forecasts |
| **fossilFuelReliance** | country | [Our World in Data energy mix](https://ourworldindata.org/energy-mix); share of energy from fossil fuels, inverted |
| **healthcareQuality** | country | WHO health system performance; [Bloomberg Health System Efficiency Index](https://www.bloomberg.com/graphics/best-and-worst/) |
| **educationQuality** | country | [PISA 2022](https://www.oecd.org/pisa/) country mean scores; QS World University Rankings city presence |
| **culturalOfferings** | city | Michelin Guide presence; UNESCO World Heritage Sites nearby; museum density; [Time Out City Index](https://www.timeout.com/city-index) |
| **walkability** | city | [Walk Score](https://www.walkscore.com/) for US/Canada cities; urban density, mixed-use zoning, and metro coverage elsewhere |
| **foodScene** | city | Michelin star count; [Time Out Food rankings](https://www.timeout.com/food-and-drink); street food culture; culinary diversity |
| **nightlife** | city | [Time Out Nightlife rankings](https://www.timeout.com/nightlife); club/bar density; 24-hour culture |
| **naturalBeauty** | city | Access to national parks, coastlines, and mountains; green space per capita; scenic landscape quality |
| **jobMarket** | city | [LinkedIn Economic Graph](https://economicgraph.linkedin.com/); IMF city employment data; startup ecosystem presence |

## Scoring algorithm

The final score for a city is a weighted average across all active dimensions:

```
effectiveWeight = baseWeight × rankMultiplier × quizMultiplier

score = sum(effectiveWeight × dimScore) / sum(effectiveWeight)
```

- **baseWeight** — from `dimensions.csv` (living vs. visiting mode)
- **rankMultiplier** — 2.0 for your top-ranked dimension, tapering to 0.1 for lowest (positions 0–7: 2.0→1.3, 8–15: 1.0→0.65, 16+: 0.5→0.1)
- **quizMultiplier** — quiz answers can boost specific dimensions (e.g. choosing "career-focused" boosts job market weight)
- **globalMultiplier** — applied after: climate mismatch (×0.7), language barrier (×0.8–0.9), language match bonus (×1.1), industry mismatch (×0.7)

See `js/scoring.js` for the full implementation.
